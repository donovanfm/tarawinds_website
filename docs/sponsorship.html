Tara Winds Sponsorship

You can help Tara Winds continue its mission to provide free concerts to the Atlanta area by becoming a sponsor today!

Your sponsorship will help Tara Winds secure rehearsal space and concert venues, purchase the rights to music, and other administrative costs associated with running a community band. Tara Winds is a 501(c)(3) organization, and all contributions are likely tax deductible, depending on your situation.

Additionally, you can donate to the Tara Winds Scholarship Fund as a way to support Tara Winds' mission of promoting music education for middle school and high school students.

Become a Sponsor (2024-2025 Season)

Step 1: Registration

To become a Tara Winds sponsor, first tell us who you are. Send us an email at sponsorship@tarawinds.org with your name and the dollar amount you'd like to contribute. Depending on your sponsorship tier, we may reach back out for additional information.

Step 2: Payment

Check back later to learn how to contribute to Tara Winds' next season.

Sponsorship Benefits

At each level, you will also receive the benefits of each lower level. For example, becoming a Gold Sponsor entitles you to the Gold benefits, as well as the Silver and Bronze benefits.

Bronze - $100

  • Recognition on the Tara Winds website
  • Recognition on all digital concert programs

Silver - $250

  • Logo on the Tara Winds homepage
  • Framed picture of the band

Gold - $1,000

  • On-stage recognition at one of our concerts
  • A social media shout out for your business

Platinum - $5,000

  • Dinner with a Tara Winds conductor

Diamond - $10,000

Your choice of one of the following:

  • A Tara Winds chamber ensemble performance at your event
  • The opportunity to guest-conduct a musical selection at a Tara Winds concert

About Tara Winds

Tara Winds is an all-adult symphonic band celebrating over 30 years of excellence in musical performance. Our founders, Larry Volman, Ed Davis, and David Gregory envisioned Tara Winds to be a performance medium for local musicians and to encourage school band members to continue performing after completion of their formal studies.
